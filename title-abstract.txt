Title:
<Agentic Privacy Control Center>

Abstract:
<Privacy by Design has become central to regulatory and design discourse, yet
operationalizing privacy principles in practice remains labor-intensive, retro-
spective, and costly. Traditional privacy reviews—such as expert audits and
impact assessments lack dynamism and do not scale well to rapidly evolving
digital platforms. We present the Agentic Privacy Control Center, an end-to-
end system that automatically discovers, structures, visualizes, and actuates
privacy settings across online platforms.
Our system integrates deterministic interface crawling, vision-based setting
extraction, semantic classification, and a planner–executor–verifier browser
agent powered by large language models. The pipeline produces a normal-
ized cross-platform privacy settings database that supports interactive visual-
ization and safe, centralized setting modification.
We demonstrate that agentic AI can reliably navigate heterogeneous privacy
interfaces, detect confirmation flows, and modify settings across platforms
without platform-specific hard-coding. Our results suggest that agentic sys-
tems can function as scalable intermediaries for privacy analysis and configu-
ration, supporting both comparative research and practitioner-facing privacy
design workflows.>
